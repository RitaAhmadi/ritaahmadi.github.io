About
Me

Please contact me!

I am a last-year DPhil student in quantum computing at Quantum Group, lead by Samson Abramsky and Bob Coecke, based at the Department of Computer Science, University of Oxford. Prior to Oxford, I worked as a master's student in Quantum Group based at Sharif University of Technology, lead by Vahid Karimipour. Before Sharif, I did my B.Sc. in Physics at Shahid Beheshti University.

Research
Interests

Reading the appendix of Kitaev 2006 during my master's, I was intrigued by category theory, which Kitaev used to describe topological excitations called Anyons. Later I wrote my master thesis on categorical aspects of topological quantum computation. So I joined Oxford to continue my studies on topological quantum field theory which moved me towards higher categories, the ZX-calculus and still topological quantum computing.
I wrote a paper on typing tensors in 2-categories and a review paper on monoidal 2-categories.
I am also interested in Machine Learning, particularily Deep Learning methods in quantum physics. As a physics enthusiast, I am also a fan of complex systems, meaning "systems composed of many components which may interact with each other." That definition includes, social systems, as well.

Teaching
Experience

Here I only list my teaching experiences at the University of Oxford. I tutored topological quantum matter at the Department of Physics, quantum mechanics at Mansfield college, category theory, and categorical quantum computing at the Department of Computer Science.

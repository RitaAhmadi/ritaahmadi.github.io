About
Me

Please contact me!

I am a last-year DPhil student in quantum computing at Quantum Group, lead by Samson Abramsky and Bob Coecke, based at the Department of Computer Science, University of Oxford. Prior to Oxford, I worked as a master's student in Quantum Group based at Sharif University of Technology, lead by Vahid Karimipour. Before Sharif, I did my B.Sc. in Physics at Shahid Beheshti University.

Research
Interests

Reading the appendix of Kitaev 2006 during my master's, I was intrigued by category theory, which Kitaev used to describe topological excitations called Anyons. Later I wrote my master thesis on categorical aspects of topological quantum computation. So I joined Oxford to continue my studies on topological quantum field theory which moved me towards higher categories, the ZX-calculus and still topological quantum computing.
I wrote a paper on typing tensors in 2-categories and a review paper on monoidal 2-categories.
I am also interested in Machine Learning, particularily Deep Learning methods in quantum physics. As a physics enthusiast, I am also a fan of complex systems, meaning "systems composed of many components which may interact with each other." This definition includes, social and financial systems, as well.

  • Oxbridge Women in Computer Science Conference

    March 2019
    The 6th version of the conference was one of the most successful events organized by the society. We had the honor of having Dame Stephanie Shirly as one of our keynote speakers. The conference had two sessions: the morning session started with a keynote speech by Rebecca Eynon followed by students' presentations highlighting 𝘸𝘰𝘮𝘦𝘯 "𝘐𝘕" 𝘤𝘰𝘮𝘱𝘶𝘵𝘦𝘳 𝘴𝘤𝘪𝘦𝘯𝘤𝘦, and the afternoon session was "𝘈𝘉𝘖𝘜𝘛" 𝘸𝘰𝘮𝘦𝘯 𝘪𝘯 𝘤𝘰𝘮𝘱𝘶𝘵𝘦𝘳 𝘴𝘤𝘪𝘦𝘯𝘤𝘦 starting with the motivational talk by Mrs. "Shirley." The conference ended with a panel discussion in the format of the initial remark and moderated by Shomon Whitson. Panel members from Academia were Marta Kwiatkowska, Blanca Rodriguez, Cristina Manolescu(Bloomberg)and from Industry were Google representative, Jan Beitner(BCS Gamma)and Cristina Manolescu(Bloomberg). The conference was funded by Google, Bloomberg, BCG Gamma, and Apple. For more information check out the website. I was responsible for:
    1. planning the conference program,
    2. inviting Keynote speakers and panel members,
    3. doing an extensive study about the topic of the panel and collaborating with the panel moderator to design the panel and write the questions for the panel members,
    4. in collaboration with our secretary, liaising, and negotiating on practical matters including sponsorship, booking the conference venue, dinner venue, organizing lunch and conference dinner.
